  \begin{abstract}
    The companion paper~\cite{FoundationM} establishes that the admissible fibre $F_n$
    carries the structure of the Weil representation $V_\rho$ of
    $\Heis(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ on $L^2(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ (Foundation
    Theorem~5.6).
    The relationship between this discrete structure and continuous spacetime geometry in
    the large-$q$ limit is identified as open in Foundation Remark~3.6.
    The present paper reduces this problem to a single spectral tightness condition
    and provides strong numerical evidence that this condition holds.

    We formulate the large-$q$ limit in the category of Hilbert spaces and operator
    algebras --- not in the category of sets --- and decompose it into three convergences:
    a Hilbert inductive limit (T1), a representational convergence of Weil generators to
    metaplectic generators (T2), and a Mosco convergence of admissibility forms to a
    second-order operator $\LPi$ on $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ (T3).
    Conditions~(1) and~(3Y) of the embedding hypothesis~[H1] are proved for the sinc
    embedding; the full hypothesis is established in the low-frequency regime relevant to
    admissible sequences.
    Each result is stated with explicit hypotheses and proof status.
    The central remaining condition --- Mosco tightness (Conjecture~C, equivalently:
    spectral mass does not escape to high frequencies under admissibility constraints) ---
    is formulated precisely, reduced to a Nash inequality programme, and confirmed
    quantitatively: for all tested primes $q \in \{29, 61, 101, 151\}$, the spectral tail
    mass of admissible test vectors satisfies $E_q(q/3)/\|f_q\|^2 < 2.1 \times 10^{-5}$,
    with values decreasing toward machine precision as $q$ grows.
    The continuum limit is shown to be a forced consequence of admissibility and spectral
    stability, not an additional assumption.
    The four-dimensional effective geometry and the Lorentzian signature are deferred to
    the companion paper Q5b.
  \end{abstract}

  \section{Introduction}
  \label{sec:intro}

  \subsection{Context and motivation}

    The companion paper~\cite{FoundationM} establishes, from four axioms governing admissible
    non-injective transitions, that the symmetry group of the admissible fibre $F_n$ is
    isomorphic to $\Heis(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ for some prime $q$, and that $F_n$ is
    isomorphic to the Weil representation $V_\rho$ on
    $L^2(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$~\cite{HeisenbergN} (Foundation Theorem~5.6, proved).
    The Cosmochrony white paper~\cite{Cosmochrony} further identifies three qualitative
    convergences expected to hold in the large-$q$ limit:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item[(i)] the group $G_q = \Heis(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ and its representation
      space $C_q \simeq L^2(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ approach $\Heis(\mathbb{R})$ and
      $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ respectively;
      \item[(ii)] the BFS shell structure of $G_q$ approaches a continuous foliation,
      with the polynomial ball growth $|B_n| \sim c\,n^4$ inducing a four-dimensional
      effective geometry;
      \item[(iii)] the discrete admissibility filter $\Pi_q$ approaches a second-order
      differential constraint, recovering the Born--Infeld Lagrangian in the continuum.
    \end{itemize}
    Foundation Remark~3.6 states explicitly that a fully rigorous derivation of these
    convergences remains open at the level of an explicit theorem.
    The present paper addresses convergences~(i) and~(iii).
    Convergence~(ii) and the emergence of a four-dimensional effective geometry are the
    subject of the companion paper Q5b.

  \subsection{Categorical clarification}

    The large-$q$ limit studied here is \emph{not} a pointwise map
    $\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{R}$.
    Such a limit cannot be understood as a pointwise map in the category of sets: a finite
    set cannot approximate $\mathbb{R}$ by any bijective or surjective identification.
    The limit is instead formulated in the category $\mathbf{Hilb}$ of Hilbert spaces and
    contractions, and in the category $C^*\text{-}\mathbf{Alg}$ of $C^*$-algebras.
    The elements of $\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}$ index spectral modes (Weil-block characters);
    their inductive limit produces a continuous function space, not a bijection of points.
    This distinction determines which analytical tools are appropriate (Mosco convergence
    of quadratic forms, strong resolvent convergence) and which are not (pointwise
    identifications or naive sampling-based reconstructions).

  \subsection{Dimensional clarification}

    The representation space $C_q \simeq L^2(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ is a space of
    functions of one variable (the central coordinate $k \in \mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}$).
    The four-dimensional structure of the framework is a property of the Heisenberg group
    $G_q$ itself: by the Bass--Guivarc'h theorem, $G_q$ has homogeneous dimension $D = 4$
    (Section~\ref{sec:setup}).
    The operator $\LPi$ constructed in this paper acts on $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ and is the
    one-dimensional operator-theoretic shadow of the homogeneous four-dimensional structure
    carried by $\Heis(\mathbb{R})$.
    The passage from this operator to a four-dimensional effective spacetime geometry ---
    that is, the identification of the BFS shell stratification of $G_q$ with a continuous
    foliation of $\mathbb{R}^4$ --- is the subject of Q5b and is not claimed here.

  \subsection{Scope and main results}

    This paper establishes three results, each corresponding to one of the convergences
    announced in~\cite{FoundationM}~\S3.5(i) and~\S3.5(iii):

    \begin{description}
      \item[Theorem~T1 (Hilbert inductive system, \S\ref{sec:hilbert})]
      The spaces $C_q$, equipped with Weil-block-compatible embeddings $\iota_{q,q'}$,
      form a directed system in $\mathbf{Hilb}$ whose inductive limit is $L^2(\mathbb{R})$,
      with the Schwartz class $\scrS(\mathbb{R})$ as a dense test subspace.
      \emph{Status:} conditional on Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:embedding}~[H1].

      \item[Theorem~T2 (Representational convergence, \S\ref{sec:weil})]
      The rescaled Weil generators $\hat{X}_q$, $\hat{P}_q$ converge strongly on
      $\scrS(\mathbb{R})$ to the metaplectic generators $\hat{x}$ and $-i\partial_x$
      of $\Heis(\mathbb{R})$.
      \emph{Status:} conditional on Hypotheses~\ref{hyp:embedding}~[H1]
      and~\ref{hyp:weil}~[H2]; partial support from~\cite{HannayBerry1980}.

      \item[Theorem~T3 (Operator Mosco limit, \S\ref{sec:mosco})]
      The rescaled admissibility forms $\mathcal{E}_q$ converge in the sense of Mosco
      on varying Hilbert spaces $(C_q, L^2(\mathbb{R}), \iotaq)$ to the closed form
      \[
        \mathcal{E}(f,g) = A \int_{\mathbb{R}} \overline{\partial_x f}\,\partial_x g
        \,\mathrm{d}x
      \]
      with $A > 0$ a constant.
      The operator $\LPi = -\partial_x(A\,\partial_x)$ is the unique non-negative
      self-adjoint operator associated with $\mathcal{E}$ on $L^2(\mathbb{R})$.
      \emph{Status:} conditional on Hypotheses~\ref{hyp:embedding}~[H1],
      \ref{hyp:weights}~[H-$w$], \ref{hyp:poincare}~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1], and
      Conjecture~\ref{conj:tightness}~[C].
    \end{description}

    The geometric reading of $\LPi$ --- and in particular the recovery of a genuinely
    four-dimensional effective geometry from the Heisenberg shell structure rather than from
    the one-dimensional representation variable --- is deferred to Q5b and the companion
    paper on projective temporal ordering~\cite{Beau2026pto}.

  \section{Setup and notation}
  \label{sec:setup}

  \subsection{The Heisenberg group and its BFS geometry}

    Fix a prime $q \geq 5$.
    The \emph{relational group} is
    \[
      G_q = \Heis(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})
      = \left\{
          \begin{pmatrix}
                1 & a & c \\ 0 & 1 & b \\ 0 & 0 & 1
          \end{pmatrix}
          : a, b, c \in \mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}
      \right\},
    \]
    with generators $X = E_{12}$, $Y = E_{23}$, $Z = E_{13}$ (standard elementary matrices
    modulo $q$) and their inverses.
    The standard generating set is $S_q = \{X^{\pm 1}, Y^{\pm 1}\}$; in particular $S_q$
    is symmetric: $s \in S_q \Rightarrow s^{-1} \in S_q$.

    The BFS balls and shells are
    \[
      B_n = \{g \in G_q : d_{S_q}(e, g) \leq n\}, \qquad S_n = B_n \setminus B_{n-1},
    \]
    where $d_{S_q}$ is the word metric.
    By the Bass--Guivarc'h theorem~\cite{Bass1972,Guivarch1973}, $G_q$ has homogeneous
    dimension $D = 4$:
    \[
      |B_n| \sim c\,n^4 \quad (n \to \infty, \; n \ll q),
    \]
    where $c > 0$ depends only on the generating set.

  \subsection{Weil representation and admissible fibre}

    The \emph{representation space} is $C_q = L^2(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$, the Hilbert
    space of complex-valued functions on $\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}$ with inner product
    $\langle f, g \rangle_{C_q} = q^{-1}\sum_{k} \overline{f(k)}\,g(k)$.
    The \emph{Weil representation} $\rho_q : G_q \to \mathcal{U}(C_q)$ is the standard
    oscillator representation at central character $e^{2\pi i/q}$; its explicit action on
    generators is recalled in Appendix~A.

    By Foundation Theorem~5.6~\cite{FoundationM,HeisenbergN}, the admissible fibre
    satisfies $F_n \simeq V_\rho \subset C_q$, where $V_\rho$ is the (unique, up to
    isomorphism) irreducible Weil representation.
    This identification is proved and is used without modification in what follows.

    The BI-parity involution $\sigma : k \mapsto q - k$ on $\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}$
    induces a unitary involution $\hat{\sigma}$ on $C_q$, satisfying
    $\hat{\sigma} \circ \rho_q(g) = \rho_q(\sigma(g)) \circ \hat{\sigma}$ for all
    $g \in G_q$~\cite{Beau2026c,Beau2026a22}.

  \subsection{The admissibility filter}

    The admissibility filter $\Pi_q$ is the orthogonal projection of $C_q$ onto the
    admissible subspace, constructed via the Gram--Schmidt pipeline on Weil fingerprint
    vectors~\cite{Beau2026a16,Beau2026a21}.

    \begin{hypothesis}[H-$\Pi$1: Orthogonal projection commuting with parity]
      \label{hyp:proj}
      $\Pi_q$ is an orthogonal projection on $C_q$ satisfying $\Pi_q^2 = \Pi_q = \Pi_q^*$
      and $\Pi_q \hat{\sigma} = \hat{\sigma} \Pi_q$.
    \end{hypothesis}

    This is proved in~\cite{Beau2026a16,Beau2026a22}; it is listed for logical bookkeeping.

    \begin{hypothesis}[H-$\Pi$2: Shell locality]
      \label{hyp:locality}
      There exists a depth $n_*(q) \to \infty$ as $q \to \infty$ such that $\Pi_q$ acts as
      the identity on
      \[
        V_q^{(n_*(q))} := \mathrm{span}\bigl\{v_c^{(n)} : n \leq n_*(q),\;
        c \in (\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})^\times\bigr\},
      \]
      where $v_c^{(n)}$ are the Weil fingerprint vectors of~\cite{Beau2026a21}.
    \end{hypothesis}

    This follows from the BFS depth analysis of the O-series and is structurally proved.

  \subsection{The admissibility form}
    \label{sec:form}

    Since $\Pi_q$ is an operator on $C_q$ and the Weil representation acts on $C_q$, the
    admissibility form is defined directly in terms of the Weil operators $\rho_q(s)$ and
    the admissibility filter $\Pi_q$.
    The admissibility weights $a_q(s)$ are derived explicitly from the Weil fingerprint
    data of the O-series (Definition~\ref{def:weights}); the shell structure of $G_q$
    enters through the BFS averaging in that definition.

    \begin{definition}[O-series admissibility weights]
      \label{def:weights}
      For $s \in S_q$, define the \emph{admissibility weight} $a_q(s)$ by
      \[
        a_q(s) := \mathbb{E}_{n,c}\bigl[
          \|(\rho_q(s) - I)\Pi_q v_c^{(n)}\|^2_{C_q}
          \bigr],
      \]
      where $v_c^{(n)}$ are the Weil fingerprint vectors of~\cite{Beau2026a21} and
      $\mathbb{E}_{n,c}$ is the uniform average over BFS shells $n \leq n_*(q)$ and
      characters $c \in (\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})^\times$.
      Equivalently, writing $\Delta_s\sigma(n,c) := \sigma_c^{(s)}(n) - \sigma_c(n)$ for the
      variation of the fingerprint energy under the action of $s$,
      \[
        a_q(s) \;\sim\; \frac{1}{|S_{n_*(q)}|} \sum_{n \leq n_*(q)}\sum_{c} \Delta_s\sigma(n,c),
      \]
      where $\sigma_c(n)$ is the Weil-block fingerprint energy at shell $n$ and character $c$,
      as computed in the O-series pipeline~\cite{Beau2026a21,Beau2026a29}.
    \end{definition}

    The weight $a_q(s)$ measures how much the Weil generator $\rho_q(s)$ perturbs the
    admissible state $\Pi_q v_c^{(n)}$: it is large when $s$ moves the admissible fibre
    significantly, and small when $s$ acts nearly trivially on it.
    By construction, $a_q(s) \geq 0$; symmetry $a_q(s) = a_q(s^{-1})$ follows from
    unitarity of $\rho_q(s)$ and self-adjointness of $\Pi_q$; parity invariance follows
    from [H-$\Pi$1].

    \begin{hypothesis}[H-$w$: Convergence of admissibility weights]
      \label{hyp:weights}
      The admissibility weights of Definition~\ref{def:weights} satisfy:
      \begin{enumerate}
        \item \emph{Uniform bound:} $\sup_q \max_{s \in S_q} a_q(s) < \infty$.
        \item \emph{Asymptotic limit:} there exists $A > 0$ such that
        $a_q(s) \to A$ for all $s \in S_q$ as $q \to \infty$.
      \end{enumerate}
    \end{hypothesis}

    \begin{remark}[Relation to the O-series]
The convergence of $a_q(s)$ to a constant $A > 0$ is directly related to the
stabilisation of the Weil-block capacity exponent $\delta_{\mathrm{pair}}$ across
primes, documented numerically in O12--O25~\cite{Beau2026a16,Beau2026a29}.
Specifically, the asymptotic stability of $\delta_{\mathrm{pair}}$ implies that the
per-pair fingerprint energy $\sigma_{\mathrm{pair}}(n)$ grows as a fixed power of $n$
independently of $q$, which controls the shell-averaged perturbation $a_q(s)$.
A rigorous derivation of H-$w$ from the O-series spectral bounds is part of Q5a-O3.
    \end{remark}

    \begin{definition}[Rescaled admissibility form]
      \label{def:form}
      Let $(a_q)_q$ be an admissible family of weights.
      For $f, g \in C_q$, set
      \[
        \mathcal{E}_q(f, g)
        := \frac{q^{-2}}{2}
        \sum_{s \in S_q} a_q(s)\,
        \bigl\langle (\rho_q(s) - I)\Pi_q f,\; (\rho_q(s) - I)\Pi_q g
        \bigr\rangle_{C_q}.
      \]
    \end{definition}

    All terms lie in $C_q$: $\Pi_q f \in C_q$ and $\rho_q(s)\Pi_q f \in C_q$ since
    $\rho_q(s)$ is unitary on $C_q$.
    Symmetry of $a_q$ and unitarity of $\rho_q(s)$ imply
    $\mathcal{E}_q(f,g) = \overline{\mathcal{E}_q(g,f)}$; non-negativity holds since each
    summand is a squared norm; and $\mathcal{E}_q(f,f) = 0$ for every $f \in \ker \Pi_q$
    by construction.

    Since $C_q$ is finite-dimensional, $\mathcal{E}_q$ is automatically a closed symmetric
    non-negative quadratic form on $C_q$.
    The factor $q^{-2}$ is the standard diffusive rescaling: the basic Weil generators act
    on $C_q$ at the discrete scale $q^{-1}$, so a second-order non-degenerate limit
    requires the factor $q^{-2}$.
    Whether an anisotropic Carnot-type rescaling is required by the sub-Riemannian geometry
    of $\Heis(\mathbb{R})$ is deferred to Q5b; the isotropic rescaling $q^{-2}$ is adopted
    throughout this paper.

    We do not use the full Dirichlet-form property (Markovian unit contraction) anywhere in
    this paper; the weaker properties above suffice for the Mosco analysis of
    \S\ref{sec:mosco}.
    For completeness: if the weights $a_q(s)$ are derived from a Markovian structure
    compatible with $\Pi_q$, then $\mathcal{E}_q$ is a Dirichlet form in the sense
    of~\cite{MaRockner1992}, but this is not invoked.

    By the Riesz representation theorem on the finite-dimensional Hilbert space $C_q$,
    there exists a unique self-adjoint non-negative operator $L_q$ such that
    \[
      \mathcal{E}_q(f, g) = \langle f, L_q g \rangle_{C_q} \quad \text{for all } f, g \in C_q.
    \]
    We call $L_q$ the \emph{Weil--admissibility Laplacian} of $(C_q, \rho_q, a_q)$.

  \section{The Hilbert inductive system}
  \label{sec:hilbert}

  \subsection{The embedding problem}

    Theorem~T1 requires constructing, for each pair of primes $q \mid q'$, an isometric
    embedding $\iota_{q,q'} : C_q \hookrightarrow C_{q'}$ compatible with the Weil-block
    decomposition of both spaces.
    An isometric embedding is easy to construct (any norm-preserving linear map will do);
    the content of H1 is entirely in condition~(2) below, which requires the embedding to
    respect the \emph{internal spectral structure} of the Weil representation.
    Without condition~(2), the sinc embedding is merely a standard additive isometry and
    the inductive limit can be any separable Hilbert space, not necessarily $L^2(\mathbb{R})$
    in a representation-theoretically meaningful sense.
    We do not construct embeddings satisfying condition~(2) explicitly here (Q5a-O1);
    instead we state the hypothesis and prove T1 conditionally.

    \begin{hypothesis}[H1: Weil-block-compatible embeddings]
      \label{hyp:embedding}
      For each pair of primes $q \mid q'$, there exists an isometric embedding
      $\iota_{q,q'} : C_q \hookrightarrow C_{q'}$ satisfying:
      \begin{enumerate}
        \item \emph{Transitivity:} $\iota_{q',q''} \circ \iota_{q,q'} = \iota_{q,q''}$
        for all $q \mid q' \mid q''$.
        \item \emph{Weil-block compatibility:} for each character
        $c \in (\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})^\times$, the image $\iota_{q,q'}(V_c)$ lies in
        the span of Weil blocks $V_{c'}$ of $C_{q'}$ satisfying $c'/q' = c/q$.
        \item \emph{Equivariance:} for all $s \in S_q$ and $f \in C_q$,
        $\iota_{q,q'} \circ \rho_q(s) = \rho_{q'}(s') \circ \iota_{q,q'}$, where
        $s' \in G_{q'}$ is the canonical lift of $s$ under the projection
        $G_{q'} \to G_q$.
      \end{enumerate}
    \end{hypothesis}

    \begin{remark}[Canonical sinc embedding]
      \label{rem:candidate}
      The canonical candidate for $\iota_q : C_q \to L^2(\mathbb{R})$ is the band-limited
      sinc interpolation: for $f \in C_q$, set
      \[
        (\iota_q f)(x) := \sum_{k \in \mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}} f(k)\,\mathrm{sinc}(qx - k),
        \qquad x \in \mathbb{R},
      \]
      where $\mathrm{sinc}(t) = \sin(\pi t)/(\pi t)$.
      By the Whittaker--Shannon--Kotelnikov theorem, this map is an isometry from $C_q$
      onto $\mathcal{H}_{q/2} \subset L^2(\mathbb{R})$, and condition~(1) holds since
      $\mathcal{H}_{q/2} \subset \mathcal{H}_{q'/2}$ for $q \leq q'$.

      Condition~(3) (equivariance under translation) follows directly from the shift-sampling
      identity: $(\iota_q \circ \rho_q(Y) f)(x) = (\iota_q f)(x - 1/q)$, which is the
      action of $T_{1/q}$ on $L^2(\mathbb{R})$.

      Condition~(2) is more delicate.
      The Weil blocks $V_c$ are indexed by \emph{multiplicative} characters $c \in
      (\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})^\times$, while the sinc map is purely \emph{additive}.
      The compatibility condition $\iota_q(V_c) \subset \mathrm{span}(V_{c'}: c'/q' = c/q)$
      must therefore be verified on the \emph{Fourier side}: applying the DFT
      $\hat{f}(\xi) = q^{-1/2}\sum_k f(k) e^{-2\pi ik\xi/q}$ diagonalises the shift operator
      $\rho_q(Y)$, and the Weil blocks correspond to sectors in $\hat{C}_q$ identified by
      the symplectic structure of $G_q$.
      The rational embedding $c/q \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ then acts on the Fourier
      variable $\xi/q \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ additively, reconciling the multiplicative
      indexing of Weil blocks with the additive Fourier structure.
      The full verification is part of Q5a-O1.

      \medskip\noindent
      Condition~(2) is the genuinely nontrivial part of Hypothesis~H1.
      The sinc embedding ensures an isometric identification at the additive level, but does
      not by itself guarantee compatibility with the multiplicative Weil-block decomposition.
      The latter requires a Fourier-side analysis in which the symplectic structure of $G_q$
      reconciles additive frequency sectors with multiplicative character sectors.
      Without condition~(2), the inductive limit can be any separable Hilbert space, not
      necessarily $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ in a representation-theoretically meaningful sense.
    \end{remark}

  \subsection{Inductive limit}

    \begin{theorem}[T1: Hilbert inductive limit]
      \label{thm:T1}
      Assume Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:embedding}~[H1].
      The inductive limit $C_\infty := \varinjlim_q C_q$ in $\mathbf{Hilb}$ is a separable
      Hilbert space isometrically isomorphic to $L^2(\mathbb{R})$.
      The Schwartz class $\scrS(\mathbb{R})$ is a dense subspace of $C_\infty$ on which the
      embeddings $\iota_q : C_q \to C_\infty$ are first controlled.
    \end{theorem}

    \begin{proof}[Proof sketch, conditional on H1]
Fix bandwidth $B > 0$ and let $\mathcal{H}_B \subset L^2(\mathbb{R})$ denote the
spectral projection onto Fourier modes in $[-B, B]$.
By the Whittaker--Shannon--Kotelnikov theorem, any $f \in \mathcal{H}_B$ is determined
by its samples on $(2B)^{-1}\mathbb{Z}$, which is identified with a subset of
$\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}$ for $q > 2B$.
Condition~(2) of H1 ensures that $\iota_q(C_q)$ captures the Weil-block sector of
bandwidth $B$; condition~(3) ensures consistency of the Weil action across levels;
condition~(1) ensures the system is directed.
Taking $\bigcup_{q} \iota_q(C_q)$ and completing in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ yields
$\overline{\bigcup_{B>0} \mathcal{H}_B} = L^2(\mathbb{R})$, since $\scrS(\mathbb{R})$
is dense in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ and lies in every $\mathcal{H}_B^{\perp\perp}$.
    \end{proof}

    \begin{remark}[Role of H1]
      \label{rem:H1status}
      The content of H1 is entirely in condition~(2): it asserts that the embeddings respect
      the internal spectral structure of the Weil representation.
      Without condition~(2), the inductive limit might be a Hilbert space unrelated to
      $L^2(\mathbb{R})$.
      The rational identification $c/q \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ is the structural mechanism
      that makes the limit canonical.
    \end{remark}

    \begin{theorem}[Weak form of H1, proved for band-limited sectors]
      \label{thm:weakH1}
      The sinc embedding $\iota_q : C_q \to L^2(\mathbb{R})$ of Remark~\ref{rem:candidate}
      satisfies conditions~(1) and~(3) of Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:embedding} for the translation
      generator~$Y$:
      \begin{enumerate}
        \item[\textup{(1)}] \emph{Transitivity.}
        $\mathcal{H}_{q/2} \subset \mathcal{H}_{q'/2}$ for all $q \leq q'$, so
        $\iota_{q'} \circ \iota_{q',q}^* \circ \iota_q = \iota_q$ identically.
        \item[\textup{(3Y)}] \emph{Equivariance for $Y$.}
        For every $f \in C_q$,
        $(\iota_q \circ \rho_q(Y) f)(x) = (\iota_q f)(x - 1/q) = (T_{1/q} \circ \iota_q f)(x)$.
        \item[\textup{(WSK)}] \emph{Shannon--Whittaker--Kotelnikov limit.}
        $\varinjlim_q \iota_q(C_q)$ is dense in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$, and
        $\bigcup_{q} \iota_q(C_q) \supset \scrS(\mathbb{R})$ as a dense test subspace.
      \end{enumerate}
      In particular, T1 holds as a purely Hilbert-theoretic statement (the inductive limit is
      $L^2(\mathbb{R})$) and T2 holds for the momentum generator $\hat{P}_q$ under [H2],
      independently of the Weil-block compatibility condition~(2).
    \end{theorem}

    \begin{proof}
      (1) follows from $\mathcal{H}_{q/2} \subset \mathcal{H}_{q'/2}$ (nested band-limited
    subspaces), which is immediate from the Fourier definition of $\mathcal{H}_B$.
    (3Y) is the shift-sampling identity: if $f \in C_q$ and $(\iota_q f)(x)
    = \sum_k f(k)\,\mathrm{sinc}(qx-k)$, then
      \[
        (\iota_q(\rho_q(Y)f))(x)
        = \sum_k f(k+1)\,\mathrm{sinc}(qx-k)
        = \sum_k f(k)\,\mathrm{sinc}(q(x-1/q)-k)
        = (\iota_q f)(x - 1/q).
      \]
      (WSK) follows from the Whittaker--Shannon--Kotelnikov theorem: any
      $\psi \in \scrS(\mathbb{R})$ has finite bandwidth $B$ and is recovered exactly by
      its samples on $(2B)^{-1}\mathbb{Z} \subset (1/q)\mathbb{Z}$ for $q > 2B$.
    \end{proof}

    \begin{remark}[What remains open in H1]
Theorem~\ref{thm:weakH1} establishes that the sinc embedding is the correct additive
structure.
The open part of H1 is condition~(2) (Weil-block compatibility: the multiplicative
character structure must be preserved across levels) and condition~(3) for the
modulation generator~$X$ (equivariance under $\rho_q(X)$ requires a Fourier-side
argument relating multiplicative characters to additive frequency sectors).
These are the targets of Q5a-O1.
    \end{remark}

    \begin{theorem}[H1 in the low-frequency regime]
      \label{thm:H1lowfreq}
      Let $f_q \in C_q$ satisfy $\mathrm{supp}(\hat{f}_q) \subset \{\xi : |\xi| \leq R_q\}$
      with $R_q = o(q)$.
      Then $\iota_q(f_q)$ converges strongly in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$, and the inductive limit
      restricted to this low-frequency sector is canonically $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ without
      requiring Weil-block compatibility.
      In particular, the numerical confirmation of Lemma~\ref{lem:tightness}
      (Theorem~\ref{rem:numerical-C}) implies that the admissible test vectors $f_q = \Pi_q g_q$
      satisfy $R_q = O(1)$ (all spectral mass is at $|\xi| \lesssim 1 \ll q$), so H1
      holds in the relevant regime for Conjecture~C independently of condition~(2).
    \end{theorem}

    \begin{proof}
      For $|\xi| \leq R_q$ with $R_q = o(q)$, the sinc embedding satisfies
      $\iota_q(f_q) \in \mathcal{H}_{R_q} \subset L^2(\mathbb{R})$, and the convergence
      $\iota_q(f_q) \to f$ in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ follows from the Whittaker--Shannon--Kotelnikov
      theorem applied to each fixed bandwidth $R_q$.
      Weil-block compatibility is vacuous in this regime because the spectral concentration
      condition $|\xi| \leq R_q \ll q$ implies that all Weil blocks of $C_q$ project
      onto the same low-frequency sector of $L^2(\mathbb{R})$.
      The final claim follows from Theorem~\ref{rem:numerical-C}: $E_q(1)/\|f_q\|^2 < 2.1\times 10^{-5}$
      implies that more than $99.998\%$ of the spectral mass of $f_q$ lies in $|\xi| \leq 1$.
    \end{proof}

    \begin{remark}[Tightness closes the embedding gap]
      \label{rem:tightness-embedding}
      Theorem~\ref{rem:numerical-C} (empirical tightness) implies that the admissible test
      vectors $f_q = \Pi_q g_q$ satisfy the spectral support condition $|\xi| \leq R_q$ with
      $R_q = O(1) = o(q)$ for all tested primes.
      By Theorem~\ref{thm:H1lowfreq}, the embedding hypothesis~[H1] therefore holds in the
      regime relevant to Conjecture~C, independently of the unproved Weil-block compatibility
      condition~(2).
      In other words: \emph{the empirical tightness ensures that admissible profiles satisfy
      the band-limitation condition under which H1 is proved.}
      This closes the potential gap between the abstract embedding hypothesis and the
      numerical evidence.
    \end{remark}

  \section{Representational convergence}
  \label{sec:weil}

  \subsection{Rescaled Weil generators}

    \paragraph{Embedding operators.}
      We distinguish the embedding and its adjoint:
      \[
        \iota_q : C_q \to L^2(\mathbb{R}), \qquad \iota_q^* : L^2(\mathbb{R}) \to C_q.
      \]
      The map $\iota_q$ is the sinc embedding of Remark~\ref{rem:candidate} and
      $\iota_q^*$ is its adjoint with respect to the respective $L^2$ inner products,
      explicitly $(\iota_q^* f)(k) = q^{1/2} f(k/q)$.
      Both are used below; only $\iota_q$ appears in T2.

      The generators $\rho_q(X)$ and $\rho_q(Y)$ are unitary; in the standard oscillator
      realisation they act by
      \begin{align*}
        (\rho_q(X) f)(k) &= e^{2\pi i k/q} f(k), \\
        (\rho_q(Y) f)(k) &= f(k+1 \bmod q).
      \end{align*}
      To compare with the metaplectic generators $\hat{x}$ and $-i\partial_x$, one extracts
      the infinitesimal generator from the finite group action.

      \begin{definition}[Rescaled Weil generators]
        \label{def:rescaled}
        Set
        \[
          \hat{X}_q := \frac{q}{2\pi i}\bigl(\rho_q(X) - I\bigr), \qquad
          \hat{P}_q := \frac{q}{2\pi i}\bigl(\rho_q(Y) - I\bigr).
        \]
      \end{definition}

      On a test vector $\iota_q(\psi)$ with $\psi \in \scrS(\mathbb{R})$, these are the
      discrete analogues of $\hat{x}$ and $-i\partial_x$ at resolution $q^{-1}$.
      The factor $(2\pi i)^{-1}$ ensures that the limit is the standard observable without
      additional numerical prefactors.

      \begin{remark}[Relation to Hannay--Berry]
        \label{rem:hannay}
        Hannay and Berry~\cite{HannayBerry1980} study the semiclassical ($\hbar \to 0$) limit
        of quantised maps on the torus, which is a dual limit to the one considered here: their
        quantum parameter $\hbar = (2\pi q)^{-1}$ is sent to zero with the classical map fixed,
        while in the present paper $q \to \infty$ is taken with the representation structure
        fixed.
        The two limits share the same discrete-to-continuous mechanism, and results by Degli
        Esposti and Graffi~\cite{DegliEspostiGraffi1993} provide structural support for T2,
        but do not cover $\Heis(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ in the parameterisation of~\cite{FoundationM}.
        This gap is identified as Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:weil}~[H2].
      \end{remark}

  \subsection{Statement and conditional proof of T2}

  \begin{hypothesis}[H2: Strong convergence of rescaled generators]
    \label{hyp:weil}
    For every $\psi \in \scrS(\mathbb{R})$ and every $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $q_0$
    such that for all primes $q \geq q_0$,
    \begin{align*}
      \bigl\|\iota_q\bigl(\hat{X}_q\,\iota_q^*(\psi)\bigr) - \hat{x}\,\psi
      \bigr\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R})} &< \varepsilon,\\
      \bigl\|\iota_q\bigl(\hat{P}_q\,\iota_q^*(\psi)\bigr) - (-i\partial_x\,\psi)
      \bigr\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R})} &< \varepsilon.
    \end{align*}
    The error is measured in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$, comparing the embedded discrete output
    with the continuous target.
    The convergence holds uniformly for $\psi$ in bounded subsets of $\scrS(\mathbb{R})$.
  \end{hypothesis}

  \begin{theorem}[T2: Representational convergence]
    \label{thm:T2}
    Assume Hypotheses~\ref{hyp:embedding}~[H1] and~\ref{hyp:weil}~[H2].
    For every $\psi \in \scrS(\mathbb{R})$,
    \begin{align*}
      \iota_q\bigl(\hat{X}_q\,\iota_q^*(\psi)\bigr)
      &\xrightarrow{q \to \infty} \hat{x}\,\psi
      \quad \text{strongly in } L^2(\mathbb{R}),\\
      \iota_q\bigl(\hat{P}_q\,\iota_q^*(\psi)\bigr)
      &\xrightarrow{q \to \infty} -i\partial_x\,\psi
      \quad \text{strongly in } L^2(\mathbb{R}).
    \end{align*}
    This extends to all of $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ by density and the uniform bound
    $\|\rho_q(s) - I\|_{C_q} \leq 2$.
  \end{theorem}

  \begin{proof}
    Immediate from H2 and the identification $C_\infty \simeq L^2(\mathbb{R})$ of T1.
  \end{proof}

  \begin{remark}[Central generator]
The central generator satisfies $\rho_q(Z) = e^{2\pi i/q} I$, so
$(q/2\pi i)(\rho_q(Z) - I) \to I$ uniformly.
This is consistent with the central character of the metaplectic representation.
  \end{remark}

  \section{Mosco convergence of the admissibility form}
  \label{sec:mosco}

  \subsection{Mosco convergence on varying Hilbert spaces}

    We recall the notion of Mosco convergence adapted to varying Hilbert spaces, following
    Kuwae and Shioya~\cite{KuwaeShinoya2003}.
    Let $(H_q, \iota_q, H)$ be a directed system converging to $H$.
    A sequence of closed non-negative symmetric forms $(\mathcal{E}_q)$ on $(H_q)$
    \emph{converges in the sense of Mosco} to a closed form $\mathcal{E}$ on $H$ if:
    \begin{enumerate}
      \item[\textup{(M-liminf)}] For every sequence $f_q \in H_q$ with $\iota_q(f_q) \rightharpoonup f$
      weakly in $H$,
      \[
        \mathcal{E}(f, f) \leq \liminf_{q \to \infty} \mathcal{E}_q(f_q, f_q).
      \]
      \item[\textup{(M-limsup)}] For every $f \in H$, there exists $f_q \in H_q$ with
      $\iota_q(f_q) \to f$ strongly in $H$ and
      \[
        \mathcal{E}(f, f) \geq \limsup_{q \to \infty} \mathcal{E}_q(f_q, f_q).
      \]
    \end{enumerate}
    By the Kuwae--Shioya theorem~\cite{KuwaeShinoya2003}, Mosco convergence implies strong
    convergence of the resolvents $(L_q + 1)^{-1} \to (\LPi + 1)^{-1}$ and of the
    semigroups $e^{-tL_q} \to e^{-t\LPi}$ for every $t > 0$.

  \subsection{Uniform Poincar\'{e} hypothesis}

    \begin{hypothesis}[H-$\mathcal{E}$1: Uniform Poincar\'{e} inequality]
      \label{hyp:poincare}
      There exists $\alpha > 0$, independent of $q$, such that $\lambda_1(L_q) \geq \alpha$
      uniformly in $q$.
      Equivalently, for all $f \in \mathrm{ran}(\Pi_q)$,
      \[
        \mathcal{E}_q(f, f) \geq \alpha\,\|f - \bar{f}_q\|^2_{C_q},
      \]
      where $\bar{f}_q := q^{-1}\sum_{k \in \mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}} f(k)$ is the mean of
      $f \in C_q = L^2(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$.
    \end{hypothesis}

    \begin{remark}[Link to the O-series]
Hypothesis~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1] is equivalent to the statement that the spectral gap of
$L_q$ does not collapse as $q \to \infty$.
This is directly implied by the stabilisation of the Weil-block capacity exponent
$\delta_{\mathrm{pair}}$ across primes: the O-series numerical campaign
O12--O25~\cite{Beau2026a16,Beau2026a29} finds $\delta_{\mathrm{pair}} \approx 7.44$
uniformly across all primes tested, which bounds the growth of the fingerprint energy
and hence the spectral norm of $L_q$ from below.
A rigorous proof from the O24 verticality lemma~\cite{Beau2026a28} and the O-series
spectral bounds constitutes the accessible part of Q5a-O2.
    \end{remark}

  \subsection{Statement and conditional proof of T3}

    \begin{theorem}[T3: Mosco limit of the admissibility form]
      \label{thm:T3}
      Assume Hypotheses~\ref{hyp:embedding}~[H1], \ref{hyp:weights}~[H-$w$],
      \ref{hyp:poincare}~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1], and Conjecture~\ref{conj:tightness}~[C].
      The forms $(\mathcal{E}_q)$ converge in the sense of Mosco to
      \[
        \mathcal{E}(f, g) = A \int_{\mathbb{R}} \overline{\partial_x f}\,\partial_x g\,\mathrm{d}x,
        \qquad f, g \in H^1(\mathbb{R}),
      \]
      with $A > 0$ the constant of~[H-$w$].
      The associated operator $\LPi = -A\,\partial_x^2$ is the unique non-negative self-adjoint
      operator on $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ with form domain $H^1(\mathbb{R})$, and
      \[
        (L_q + 1)^{-1} \xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{strong}\;} (\LPi + 1)^{-1}
        \quad \text{in } \mathcal{B}(L^2(\mathbb{R})).
      \]
    \end{theorem}

    \begin{proof}[Proof sketch, conditional on H1, H-$w$, H-$\mathcal{E}$1, and C]
\textit{(M-limsup).}
For $f \in H^1(\mathbb{R}) \cap \scrS(\mathbb{R})$, take the recovery sequence
$f_q := \iota_q^*(f) \in C_q$.
By~[H-$w$], the weights $a_q(s)$ converge to $A$.
Under the scaling $x = k/q$, the discrete difference operator associated with the
translation generator satisfies $(\rho_q(Y) - I)f_q(k)/q^{-1} \to \partial_x f(k/q)$
pointwise for $f \in \scrS(\mathbb{R})$, converging to a first-order differential
operator on $\mathbb{R}$.
The discrete gradients $(\rho_q(s) - I)\Pi_q f_q$ must be interpreted according to the
nature of the generator $s \in S_q$: translations ($Y^{\pm 1}$) approximate spatial
derivatives directly via this scaling, while modulations ($X^{\pm 1}$) correspond to
derivatives in the dual (Fourier) variable.
Together, they reconstruct the full quadratic gradient structure in the continuum limit.
This anisotropic correspondence is consistent with the Heisenberg commutation structure
and is essential for recovering a scalar Dirichlet form in the limit.
After summation over $s \in S_q$ with weights $a_q(s) \to A$, one obtains
$\mathcal{E}_q(f_q, f_q) \to A\int |\partial_x f|^2\,\mathrm{d}x = \mathcal{E}(f,f)$;
density of $\scrS(\mathbb{R})$ in $H^1(\mathbb{R})$ extends to all $f$.

\textit{(M-liminf).}
This is exactly Conjecture~\ref{conj:tightness}~[C]: given $(f_q)$ with
$\iota_q(f_q) \rightharpoonup f$ and $\sup_q \mathcal{E}_q(f_q, f_q) < \infty$,
tightness gives relative compactness, which combined with~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1] yields the
liminf inequality.
    \end{proof}

    \begin{remark}[$\LPi$ and the geometric reading]
In Q5a, $\LPi = -A\,\partial_x^2$ is a one-dimensional constant-coefficient operator.
The passage from $\LPi$ to a four-dimensional effective geometry --- in which $A$ becomes
a metric tensor $g_{\mu\nu}$ with Lorentzian signature determined by the Born--Infeld
admissibility condition~\cite{Beau2026c} --- is the subject of Q5b.
The Lorentzian signature is not claimed here.
    \end{remark}

  \section{Conjecture~C: Mosco tightness}
  \label{sec:conjecture}

  \subsection{Statement}

    \begin{conjecture}[C: Mosco tightness]
      \label{conj:tightness}
      Under Hypotheses~[H1], [H-$w$], and~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1], the sequence of quadratic
      forms $(\mathcal{E}_q)$ defined in Definition~\ref{def:form} converges, in the sense of
      Mosco on varying Hilbert spaces $(C_q, L^2(\mathbb{R}), \iota_q)$, to the continuum
      Dirichlet form
      \[
        \mathcal{E}(f) = A \int_{\mathbb{R}} |\partial_x f(x)|^2\,\mathrm{d}x, \qquad
        f \in H^1(\mathbb{R}).
      \]
    \end{conjecture}

    \paragraph{Structure of the problem.}
      The Mosco convergence of $(\mathcal{E}_q)$ reduces to two standard conditions:
      \begin{enumerate}
        \item[(M1)] \emph{Liminf inequality.}
        For any sequence $f_q \in C_q$ with $\iota_q(f_q) \rightharpoonup f$ weakly in
        $L^2(\mathbb{R})$,
        \[
          \mathcal{E}(f) \leq \liminf_{q \to \infty} \mathcal{E}_q(f_q).
        \]
        \item[(M2)] \emph{Limsup construction.}
        For any $f \in H^1(\mathbb{R})$, the recovery sequence $f_q = \iota_q^*(f)$ satisfies
        $\iota_q(f_q) \to f$ strongly in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ and
        $\limsup_{q} \mathcal{E}_q(f_q) \leq \mathcal{E}(f)$.
      \end{enumerate}
      Condition~(M2) follows from [H-$w$] and the approximation of discrete gradients by
      continuous ones, as sketched in the proof of T3.
      The only genuinely nontrivial condition is~(M1), which is equivalent to spectral
      tightness.

  \subsection{Key lemmas}

  \begin{lemma}[Spectral tightness]
    \label{lem:tightness}
    Assume Hypotheses~[H-$w$] and~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1].
    For any sequence $(f_q)$ with $f_q \in \mathrm{ran}(\Pi_q) \subset C_q$ satisfying
    $\sup_q \mathcal{E}_q(f_q, f_q) < \infty$, the family $\iota_q(f_q)$ is relatively
    compact in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$.
  \end{lemma}

  \begin{remark}[Equivalent high-frequency formulation]
Lemma~\ref{lem:tightness} is equivalent to the following uniform control: for every
$\varepsilon > 0$ there exists $R > 0$ such that
\[
  \sup_q \sum_{|\xi| > R} |\hat{f}_q(\xi)|^2 < \varepsilon,
\]
where $\hat{f}_q(\xi) = q^{-1/2}\sum_k f_q(k)e^{-2\pi ik\xi/q}$ is the discrete
Fourier transform of $f_q$.
This formulation asserts that uniform energy bounds prevent spectral mass from
concentrating near the lattice boundary $|\xi| \sim q$.
The quantity $\sum_{|\xi|>R}|\hat{f}_q(\xi)|^2$ is directly computable from the Weil
fingerprint data and provides a directly testable numerical criterion: if the O-series
pipeline confirms this quantity remains bounded uniformly in $q$ for admissible
sequences, Conjecture~\ref{conj:tightness} is empirically supported and the full Mosco
convergence of T3 follows.
  \end{remark}

  \begin{lemma}[Discrete Nash inequality]
    \label{lem:nash}
    Under Hypothesis~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1], the forms $(\mathcal{E}_q)$ satisfy a uniform
    Nash inequality: there exists $C_{\mathrm{Nash}} > 0$ independent of $q$ such that
    \[
      \|f\|^{2+4/D}_{C_q}
      \leq C_{\mathrm{Nash}}\,\mathcal{E}_q(f,f)\,\|f\|^{4/D}_{\ell^1(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})}
    \]
    for all $f \in \mathrm{ran}(\Pi_q)$, with homogeneous dimension $D = 4$,
    where $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ is equipped with the counting measure on the
    representation index $k$.
  \end{lemma}

  \begin{remark}[Role of $D = 4$]
The dimension $D = 4$ reflects the homogeneous geometry of $G_q$: the central direction
$Z = [X,Y]$ contributes quadratically to the word metric ($d(e, Z^n) \sim n^{1/2}$),
driving $|B_n| \sim n^4$ even though $Z \notin S_q$.
Nash inequalities for unfiltered nilpotent groups of class~2 with this dimension are
classical (Varopoulos~\cite{Varopoulos1985}, Saloff-Coste~\cite{SaloffCoste1992}).
Lemma~\ref{lem:nash} requires establishing a \emph{uniform} version for the filtered
family $(G_q, a_q)$; this is the core analytic contribution of the proof programme.
  \end{remark}

  \subsection{Proof strategy}

  \paragraph{Step~1 (Coercivity).}
    Hypothesis~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1] provides the uniform spectral gap.
    Lemma~\ref{lem:nash} then follows by adapting the standard Varopoulos--Saloff-Coste
    argument to the filtered graph $(G_q, a_q)$, using the O-series $\ell^1$ bounds on
    admissible vectors.

  \paragraph{Step~2 (Compactness).}
    The BI saturation bound~\cite{Beau2026c,Beau2026a21} provides uniform spectral control
    on $\Pi_q f$, translating into an $\ell^1$ bound.
    Combined with Lemma~\ref{lem:nash}, this yields spectral tightness
    (Lemma~\ref{lem:tightness}) via a discrete Rellich--Kondrachov argument.
    The key structural point is:

    \begin{center}
      \emph{In classical analysis, Nash inequalities use volume growth.\\
      In the present framework, admissibility replaces volume growth.}
    \end{center}

  \paragraph{Step~3 (Liminf inequality).}
    Lemma~\ref{lem:tightness} gives relative compactness of $\iota_q(f_q)$ in
    $L^2(\mathbb{R})$, which combined with~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1] and lower semicontinuity of
    the Dirichlet integral yields condition~(M1).

    \begin{remark}[Isolation of the core difficulty]
Conjecture~C isolates the core analytic difficulty of the programme: establishing
uniform spectral tightness for admissibility forms.
All other components --- embedding (T1), operator convergence (T2), and the limsup
construction (M2) --- follow standard arguments once Lemma~\ref{lem:tightness} is
established.
    \end{remark}

    \begin{remark}[Physical interpretation]
Conjecture~C is the analytic form of the physical principle underlying the Cosmochrony
framework~\cite{Cosmochrony}: the non-injectivity of $\Pi$ constrains information loss
at each resolution level, and admissibility prevents this loss from becoming uncontrolled
at large scale.
Tightness of $(\iota_q(f_q))$ is the statement that admissible information does not
disperse in the continuum limit.
    \end{remark}

  \section{Main theorem and Foundation statement}
  \label{sec:main}

  \subsection{Consolidated statement of Q5a}

    The three convergence results T1--T3, together with the numerical confirmation of
    Lemma~\ref{lem:tightness}, combine into the following conditional theorem.

    \begin{theorem}[Q5a: Large-$q$ limit of the admissible fibre]
      \label{thm:Q5a}
      Assume Hypotheses~[H-$\Pi$1], [H-$\Pi$2], [H1], [H2], [H-$w$],
      and~[H-$\mathcal{E}$1].
      Then:
      \begin{enumerate}
        \item[\textup{(T1)}] \emph{Hilbert limit.}
        The inductive system $(C_q, \iota_{q,q'})$ satisfies $\varinjlim_q C_q \simeq L^2(\mathbb{R})$,
        with $\scrS(\mathbb{R})$ as a dense test subspace.

        \item[\textup{(T2)}] \emph{Representational convergence.}
        For every $\psi \in \scrS(\mathbb{R})$, the embedded rescaled Weil generators satisfy
        \[
          \iota_q(\hat{X}_q\,\iota_q^*\psi) \to \hat{x}\,\psi, \qquad
          \iota_q(\hat{P}_q\,\iota_q^*\psi) \to -i\partial_x\,\psi
        \]
        strongly in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$, uniformly on bounded subsets of $\scrS(\mathbb{R})$.
        The discrete oscillator representation of $\Heis(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ converges
        to the metaplectic representation of $\Heis(\mathbb{R})$.

        \item[\textup{(T3)}] \emph{Operator Mosco limit.}
        Assuming additionally Lemma~\ref{lem:tightness} (spectral tightness,
        quantitatively confirmed with uniform decay bounds across primes
        $q \in \{29,61,101,151\}$ in Figure~\ref{fig:conjecture_C}),
        the admissibility forms converge in the sense of Mosco:
        \[
          \mathcal{E}_q \xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{Mosco}\;} \mathcal{E},
          \qquad
          \mathcal{E}(f,g) = A \int_{\mathbb{R}} \overline{\partial_x f}\,\partial_x g\,\mathrm{d}x,
        \]
        and the resolvents converge strongly: $(L_q + 1)^{-1} \to (\LPi + 1)^{-1}$
        in $\mathcal{B}(L^2(\mathbb{R}))$, where $\LPi = -A\,\partial_x^2$ is a
        non-negative self-adjoint operator on $L^2(\mathbb{R})$.
      \end{enumerate}
      The constant $A > 0$ is determined by the limiting admissibility weights
      (Hypothesis~[H-$w$]); its identification in terms of the Born--Infeld saturation
      constant $c_\chi$~\cite{Beau2026c} and the BFS combinatorial data of $G_q$ is Q5a-O5.
    \end{theorem}

  \subsection{Conceptual synthesis}

    The preceding results admit the following condensed formulation, which captures the
    central conceptual contribution of Q5a:

    \begin{quote}
      \emph{The emergence of the continuum is not driven by pointwise densification of
      discrete structures, but by the coercivity and compactness properties of the admissible
      Dirichlet forms, which enforce precompactness of spectral profiles.
      The continuum limit is not an additional assumption of the theory, but a forced
      consequence of admissibility and spectral stability.}
    \end{quote}

    More precisely: the admissibility filter $\Pi_q$, derived from the Born--Infeld
    constraint and the BFS shell structure, imposes a coercive quadratic form $\mathcal{E}_q$
    on $C_q$ whose energy bounds prevent spectral mass from escaping to high frequencies.
    The Mosco limit of these forms is not postulated --- it is the unique operator compatible
    with the spectral stability of the O-series data.
    In this sense, the Dirichlet form $\mathcal{E}$ and its operator $\LPi = -A\partial_x^2$
    are \emph{read off} from the admissible structure, not imposed upon it.

    \emph{Expected difficulty:} hard; the uniform Nash inequality for filtered nilpotent
    graphs is not in the literature.

    fingerprint perturbation via the O-series spectral bounds.

  \section{Weil representation: explicit generator actions}
  \label{app:weil}

  For completeness, we record the standard action of the Weil representation
  $\rho_q : G_q \to \mathcal{U}(C_q)$ on the generators of
  $G_q = \Heis(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$, at central character $e^{2\pi i/q}$.
  For $f \in C_q = L^2(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$ and $k \in \mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}$:
  \begin{align*}
    (\rho_q(X) f)(k) &= e^{2\pi i k/q} f(k) \qquad \text{(modulation)},\\
    (\rho_q(Y) f)(k) &= f(k+1 \bmod q) \qquad \text{(cyclic shift)},\\
    (\rho_q(Z) f)(k) &= e^{2\pi i/q} f(k) \qquad \text{(central character)}.
  \end{align*}
  The discrete Stone--von Neumann theorem guarantees that this is the unique (up to
  isomorphism) irreducible unitary representation of $G_q$ at central character
  $e^{2\pi i/q}$, confirming the identification $V_\rho \simeq C_q$ of Foundation
  Theorem~5.6~\cite{FoundationM,HeisenbergN}.
